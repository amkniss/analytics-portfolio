Where the Money Is Going

~€78,800 leaves every month.
Here's exactly where.

01
Country Selector Overlay
Render-blocking full-screen gate firing before page paint
€10,080
/month est.

The full-screen country selector fires before the homepage fully paints — it's the first thing 100% of new visitors encounter. This forces a decision from someone who hasn't yet seen a product or understood the brand. Industry data shows this type of overlay increases bounce rate by 12–18% in the first session. At your mobile traffic volume (~60% of sessions), this renders a meaningful share of mobile visits unconverted before any product interaction occurs. The fix: replace with geo-IP auto-detection and a subtle header flag.

How this is calculated: Base = €14.4M annual revenue (£12M × 1.20) × 70% DTC channel × 60% mobile traffic ÷ 12 months = €504,000/month mobile base. Industry benchmark: render-blocking full-screen overlay drives 12–18% bounce increase. Conservative net CVR recovery modeled at 2% = €504,000 × 2% = €10,080/month.

Google's research confirms that every 100ms delay in mobile page load decreases conversion by 7%. At a 45/100 score (vs. the 70+ threshold), Dr. Sam's mobile experience is delivering approximately 4.2 seconds to first meaningful content on 4G. The primary culprits: hero videos not lazy-loaded, and a likely render conflict between Cloudflare Rocket Loader and the Shogun page builder's scripts. Bringing mobile score to 70+ typically yields a meaningful CVR improvement — conservatively modeled as €30,240/month on mobile revenue.

How this is calculated: Base = €504,000/month mobile (see above). Google benchmark: every 100ms delay = 7% CVR decrease; moving from score 45 to 70+ industry benchmark = 18–22% CVR lift potential. 03
No Sticky Add-to-Cart on Mobile
ATC button buried below fold, out of thumb zone on mobile PDPs
€15,120
/month est.

On mobile PDPs, the Add-to-Cart button requires the user to scroll past the product description, ingredients list, and clinical info sections before they can add to cart — or scroll back up to the top. A sticky ATC that appears as soon as a user scrolls past the hero image is one of the most reliably proven mobile CVR improvements in DTC. Baymard Institute research and DTC case studies consistently show an 8–12% mobile CVR lift from persistent ATC accessibility. Modeled conservatively at 3% = €15,120/month.

How this is calculated: Base = €504,000/month mobile (see above). Baymard Institute + DTC case study benchmark: 8–12% mobile CVR lift from persistent ATC. Conservative recovery modeled at 3% to reflect brand-specific conditions = €504,000 × 3% = €15,120/month.

04
AI Search Invisibility Gap (GEO)
Dr. Sam Bunting cited as expert. Products not recommended. Schema missing.
€14,400
/month est.

When a user asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google SGE "what's the best dermatologist azelaic acid serum?", Dr. Sam Bunting appears as a credible voice — but the Neutralise Serum is not surfaced as a recommended product. This is not a content quality problem. The site has excellent clinical content. The issue is that MedicalEntity and FAQPage JSON-LD schema are missing — meaning AI engines can't connect the doctor's authority to the products she formulated. With 40% of beauty consumers now beginning discovery via AI search, this gap compounds monthly. Conservative estimate: €14,400/month in incremental organic revenue not being captured.

How this is calculated: AI search now accounts for an estimated 10–15% of new discovery sessions in the beauty category. Modeled at 3% of total DTC monthly revenue (€14.4M × 70% ÷ 12 = €840k/mo DTC base) currently lost due to missing GEO schema = €14,400/month. This is intentionally the most conservative line item, as AI citation uplift depends on content quality already present on this site.

Keyword Cannibalization (Blog vs. PDP)
Blog posts and product pages fighting for the same search terms

The "Expert Advice" blog posts (e.g., "How to Use Azelaic Acid") are competing directly with the Neutralise Serum PDP for terms like "Azelaic acid" and "Azelaic acid for acne." Google is choosing to rank the blog post — because it has more inbound links — which means the purchase-intent page is suppressed in organic results. The fix involves canonical tags, internal linking architecture, and H2 rewrites on PDPs. Estimated organic traffic recovery: €9,000/month in incremental purchase-intent sessions.

How this is calculated: DTC monthly base = €14.4M × 70% ÷ 12 = €840,000/month. Keyword cannibalization in a content-rich DTC brand typically suppresses 3–5 high-intent PDPs, each losing 20–30% of their organic potential. Conservative recovery modeled at ~1.1% of monthly DTC base, representing regained ranking position on 2–3 core product terms = €9,000/month.
