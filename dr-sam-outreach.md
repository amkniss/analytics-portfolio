## Usage Notes

**Send order:** Start with Email 1 (Neil) and Email 2 (Sarah) simultaneously. If no reply in 5 business days, use the LinkedIn connection request to Neil. If he accepts, send the InMail follow-up.

**Tone principle:** Every message frames the gap as something they're probably already aware of but haven't had time to dig into — not as a failure. The problems are described as "not easy to spot," "invisible on the front end," "configuration not design." This keeps the person reading on the defensive side of curious rather than defensive.

**What never to say:**
- Do not say "I noticed your site has issues" — say "I was doing research and found something"
- Do not say "you're losing revenue" in the subject — save the number for the call
- Do not say "I can help you" — say "happy to share what I found"
- Do not pitch the service in email 1 — just create enough curiosity to get the reply

**If they reply asking what you do:** "I work with DTC brands on the technical gaps that tend to emerge at the £10M+ stage — schema, mobile CVR, AI search visibility. Most of it is invisible until you're trying to double revenue."
